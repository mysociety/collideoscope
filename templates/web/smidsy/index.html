How Collideoscope works

  • Find a location by postcode, street, or landmark
  • Tell us a few details about your collision or near-miss
  • We notify the council’s highways department
  • And make statistics available to planners and campaigners

Need cycling collision data?

Collideoscope is building into a massive nationwide archive of data on cycling collisions and near-misses, which you can use for free.

Browse and download our data for campaigners, cyclists, and road safety experts

As well as our users’ reports, our statistics and maps also include police STATS19 data, for a comprehensive visualisation of cycling incidents.

Why use Collideoscope?

I cycle my daughter to school, but the pavement parking round there is a nightmare. After someone almost opened their door right into me, I reported the location on Collideoscope.

Within a few days, the council arranged for a safety officer to give a talk at the school, and said they’ll be rolling my feedback into their case for introducing a no-parking zone on the school road. Win!

As a cycling campaigner, presenting local data to local decision-makers is super valuable. Before I go to council meetings or protests, I look up the councillors’ wards on Collideoscope, so I can show them the state of cycle safety in their area. They’ve often never seen it presented so clearly! It’s a great conversation starter.

I’m a confident cyclist, but when a car driver gave me a punishment passing for claiming the lane, I was furious. They’d disappeared before I could take the licence number, but I reported it on Collideoscope, and the site automatically showed me where and how to report it to the local police. Thanks Collideoscope!
